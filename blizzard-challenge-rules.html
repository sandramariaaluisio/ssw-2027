The Blizzard Challenge 2027

Evaluating corpus-based speech synthesis systems

The Rules of the Blizzard Challenge 2027

Database Access

Refer to the Blizzard 2027 Call for Participation page.

Registration Fee

A registration fee of €500 is payable by all participating teams who wish to submit synthetic speech for evaluation, to offset the costs of running the challenge, including paying local assistants and listeners. The fee must be paid by 13th April 2027. Each fee covers the entry from one participating team; that entry can comprise either or both of the tasks below.

Under no circumstances should you pay the fee until your request to participate has been accepted by the organisers. We strongly recommend paying this fee only after you have decided to submit synthetic speech (e.g., after completing your own internal evaluation prior to submission). We cannot issue refunds.

Payment of the fee does not guarantee that your system will be included in the evaluation. We may exclude very low quality entries, at our discretion, to prevent them skewing the listening test (and wasting listener effort). Dealing with such entries still consumes resources, and therefore the entry fee will not be refunded.

To process your registration payment (€500 per team), we will issue an invoice through the ABRALIN financial system. Further details will be provided nearer the time.

Tasks

The task for 2027 is accented zero-shot TTS in spontaneous Portuguese speech. All participating teams must enter the hub task, and may choose whether to also enter the spoke task. In the following, "External data" is defined as data, of any type, that is not part of the provided database.

Hub task 2027-PH1

Submitting an entry for this task is mandatory.

Participating teams must only use the provided database and/or publicly-available external data.

No restriction on the amount of data.

Spoke task 2027-PS1

Submitting an entry for this task is optional.

Participating teams may use unrestricted data sources including any combination of the provided database and/or publicly-available external data and/or non-public external data.

No restriction on the amount of data.

For both tasks

Participating teams are recommended to use the provided database of speech and transcriptions, but this is not mandatory.

Participating teams are free to perform any additional processing that they wish on the provided data, such as enhancing audio quality, re-transcribing, selecting subsets from the data,  etc.

Participating teams may use any type of TTS model, including public implementations of existing models, their own implementations of published models, or newly-devised models for this challenge.

External Data and Models Policy

For both tasks, participants are required to use the provided audio files for training, while the use of the accompanying transcriptions is optional. The teams are free to enhance audio quality. It is possible to use only a sample from the large dataset of approximately 747 hours, relating to the 3 accents.

They are also free to incorporate additional external data, with no restrictions on the total amount of training data, regarding that for the hub task the additional datasets are publicly available.

Two suggestions of vocoders are given:

  • HiFi-GAN universal: GitHub
  • BigVGAN v2: Hugging Face

Regarding the TTS models, the groups are free to use any model not developed by the team, but published, or a new model just developed for this challenge.

Test Synthesis

Test Set Description

Large test corpus for both tasks, including several speakers, both male and female ones from the three accents focused on this challenge (São Paulo, Recife, and Portugal).

Technical Specifications
Audio format16 bits per sample
Supported Sampling Rates24kHz and 44.1kHz
Hearing Tests RequirementThe groups must send samples at 24 kHz.

Evaluation Process

Expert Listeners

Each participant must recruit at least 10 volunteer listeners with a background in speech technology or speech science.

Non-Expert Listeners

Recruit as many as possible. No professional speech synthesis knowledge required. Native speaker status not required.

Listener Recruitment

Share evaluation test link with colleagues/students, distribute through academic/professional networks, and help ensure robust evaluation results.

The evaluation will run in two pass/steps:

Pass 1

One subjective metric and one objective will be used to select the best systems:

  • Accent similarity and Speech Naturalness: Evaluated via EyetrackingMOS.
  • TTSDS2 Score: Comprises 4 evaluation factors:
    • GENERIC: Overall distributional similarity, via SSL embeddings.
    • SPEAKER: Realism of speaker identity.
    • PROSODY: Pitch, duration, and rhythm quality.
    • INTELLIGIBILITY: Uses ASR-derived features.
Pass 2

One subjective metric and two objective metrics will be used to show a detailed analysis of the best systems:

  • Accent Similarity: Evaluated using a test XAB.
  • Audio Quality: Evaluated using Mel Cepstral Distortion (MCD).
  • Speech Consistency: Analysis of multiple audio segments will be performed using the Speaker Embedding Extractor ECAPA2.

Documentation and Reporting

Paper Requirements

Regular Papers: Up to 4 pages for the main content, plus up to 2 additional pages reserved exclusively for references, acknowledgments, and disclosure of any generative AI tool use. Use the Interspeech 2027 template.

Submit to: Further details will be provided.

  • Describe system design decisions and approach.
  • Document external data/tools usage.
  • Address reproducible research checklist.
  • Author must present at workshop.
System Descriptions
  • Complete technical specification form.
  • Detail system architecture and features.
  • Facilitate cross-system comparisons.

Use of Results & Data Policy

  • Scientific inquiry: Results are intended strictly for scientific research purposes.
  • Commercial use prohibited: Commercial use of any evaluation results (including advertising) is strictly prohibited.
  • All submitted examples will be retained by the organizers.
  • A signed permission statement is required for public distribution.
  • Anonymous distribution of waveforms and benchmarking results will be practiced.

Terms and Conditions

  • This challenge is designed strictly for scientific inquiry and operates entirely based on participant honesty.
  • NOT A COMPETITION!!!
  • Results may not be used for commercial purposes or corporate advantage.
  • Participants must explicitly agree to the data retention and academic usage terms.
  • At least one author from each participating team must register and present their work at the workshop.
